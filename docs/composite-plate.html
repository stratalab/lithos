The composite model layer

Every mechanism with positive evidence lives above the token stream. Every mechanism with negative evidence lives below it.

The doctrine is unchanged: the weights hold the judgment, the composite holds the thing judgment operates on — a fact, a computation, a constraint. What moved is where the fact-composite belongs.

The decode policy fixes the support — applied first, to the raw logits, and final because every later stage (temperature, top-k, top-p) is monotone: each can only remove mass, never add it. Applying it last is equally safe and strictly worse — nucleus sampling would collapse the support onto the very token the policy is about to ban. The doctrine said “last write”; building it proved the property is mass-removal, and a test now enforces it.

R2 is immune to the entire sweep. Every negative result concerns fact retrieval into the output distribution. R2 approximates the same attention — its criterion is memory and latency, not bpb. It is a systems bet, and it can only fail for engineering reasons. That is why it goes first.

II · The fork — the one question that is ours

C-CTX · eval-only · ~$0 compute

At a 500M-scale context budget, is retrieval’s benefit on STEM reasoning destroyed by the context it displaces?

MassiveDS explains its factual wins with “the LM only needs to extract the answer,” and reports marginal benefit on reasoning for weaker models. Two causes fit that data. They predict identical benchmarks and opposite architectures — and nobody has separated them, because nobody runs a 500M with a short context window.

Cause (a)

Capability

A small model cannot integrate a retrieved fact into a multi-step derivation. It had the fact and the room, and still couldn’t use it.

→ Retrieval never serves the reasoner. Build in-context RAG for the mutability + citation surface only. No decode-loop retrieval, ever.

Cause (b)

Displacement

The passages ate the context the reasoning needed. The model could have used the fact; it no longer had room to think.

→ A context-free fact channel is the only route. kNN-LM’s weak per-token gain may win net of displacement — justified on Gate 5 (scarce resource), never on capability, and still owing Gates 1–4.

III · The composite acceptance test

All four negative results have the same shape: a gain credited to the composite that came from somewhere else. These are failure modes of attribution, not of retrieval — and they will recur for R2, TIR, and the decode policy. No composite’s number is believed until it clears all six.

The softmax-bottleneck justification for R1 is dead. Xu, Alon & Neubig folded the kNN-LM tricks back into the parametric LM “without the need for an explicit retrieval component.” The absorption test was run on our own mechanism, by someone else, and partly passed. R1 survives on the mutable clause alone — facts that change, or facts the model never saw. You cannot train on a fact you do not have.
